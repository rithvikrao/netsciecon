Welcome!

The paper submission deadline for the Seventh Annual Conference on Network Science and Economics is extended to January 31st, 2022. The conference will be held in-person at the University of Chicagoâ€™s Booth School of Business on March 18-20, 2022.

Due to the evolving COVID-19 situation, there may be some adjustments to the conference dates and modality. Please submit your work now even if you are unsure if you will be able to travel.

The first day, Friday, March 18, will be a single-track special theme session on (Mis)information in networks. The organizers especially encourage submissions within this theme. The remaining days will run with parallel sessions on all topics in the series' broader theme of networks in economics, which include:

  1. learning in networks
  2. selling information
  3. interfirm networks
  4. diffusion
  5. strategic marketing and influence maximization
  6. peer effects
  7. homophily
  8. network centrality
  9. networks in international trade
  10. networks of alliances and conflict
  11. financial networks
  12. models of network formation
  13. games on networks
  14. exchange and intermediation
  15. econometrics and statistics of networks

Papers should be submitted via email to networksciecon@gmail.com. Please indicate whether you are submitting for the first day special theme session or the open conference. A preliminary program will be announced by February 18, 2022. No conference proceedings will be published.

While extended abstracts are acceptable, full papers are encouraged. Young scholars are particularly encouraged to submit papers. Speakers' expenses will be partially reimbursed, with amounts depending on funding. The conference will make a special effort to support the attendance of junior presenters without their own travel funding.

The conference website (https://netsciecon.org/) will be updated to include information about lodging, location, and other details.

The conference is sponsored by the National Science Foundation, with additional support from the Booth School of Business and the Becker Friedman Institute, and is part of an NSF-sponsored series, Network Science in Economics. It aims to foster the development and dissemination of cutting edge research in network theory.
